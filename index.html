About

I am Jieung Kim, an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science, College of Computing at Yonsei University, South Korea. I work on realizable theory — designing safety-guaranteed systems with formal verification at its core.

Before I joined Yonsei University, I was an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Engineering, College of Software and Convergence at Inha University, South Korea.

Before that, I was a research (software) engineer — privacy & security at Cerebra (Personal AI), Google Research and the Machine Learning Model Optimization Team, Core ML, Google.

Even before that, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the FLINT group in Yale with Professor Zhong Shao, and also achieved my Ph.D. degree from the same university (Yale) in 2019. I received my Master's degree at KAIST with the advice of Professor Sukyoung Ryu, and received my Bachelor's degree from Sungkyunkwan University.

Looking for students

I am actively looking for students (undergraduate and graduate) who are interested in all techniques and research (testing, fuzzing, abstract interpretation, static analysis, formal verification) related to software (distributed systems, operating systems, and blockchain) and neural network reliability, programming-language research, system software security, and machine-learning model optimization framework techniques.
