Welcome to Jieung Kim's Page

I am Jieung Kim, an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Science, College of Computing at Yonsei University, South Korea. I am interested in designing secure-guaranteed systems using multiple formal techniques.

Before I joined Yonsei University, I was an assistant professor in the Department of Computer Engineering, College of Software and Convergence at Inha University, South Korea.

Before that, I was a research (software) engineer - privacy & security at Cerebra (Personal AI), Google Research and Machine Learning Model Optimization Team, Core ML, Google.

Even before that, I worked as a postdoctoral researcher at FLINT group in Yale with Professor Zhong Shao, and also achieved my Ph.D. degree from the same university (Yale) at 2019. I received my Master's degree at KAIST with the advise from Professor Sukyoung Ryu, and received my Bachelor's degree from Sungkyunkwan University.

I am actively looking for students (undergraduate and graduate) who are interested in all techniques and researches (testing, fuzzing, abstract interpretation, static analysis, formal verification) related to software (distributed systems, operating systems, and block chain) and neural network reliability, programming language related researches, system software securities, and machine learning model optimization framework techniques.
